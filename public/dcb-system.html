How the DCB suite fits together

One thesis, four products as one stack: Research is the knowledge layer (the grounded source of truth), Practice the execution layer (agents do the work, humans approve), Copilot the interface layer (the practitioner's screen), and Public the distribution layer (the consumer front door). Research grounds the others. Each product owns its own schema; one Postgres, isolated by role and entitlement, extractable per product when scale earns it.

💡 Why the suite exists

  • Public, general AI answers finance questions confidently wrong; the real answer sits on IRS / SEC / state sites, so it gives vetted, cited answers instead.
  • Practice, tax work is competitive & commoditised and a firm's roles split cleanly into agents, so it delivers the service itself, not another tool.
  • Copilot, you can't ship a Cursor-style sidebar inside every legacy accounting app, so it's screen-aware and spans them all.
  • Research, the backbone that funnels correct, grounded information into the other three.

↻ The loop

  • Research distills cited entries into Public and feeds Practice's firm KB with grounded how a task should be performed.
  • Copilot sends redacted work-context events to Practice via its API.
  • Inside Practice, agents retrieve how-to, do the work, humans review & approve, outcomes are recorded.
  • Approved outcomes flow back to continuously learn the firm KB.
  • Practice reads the firm's bank transactions (Plaid) to match payments to open invoices for you to confirm.
