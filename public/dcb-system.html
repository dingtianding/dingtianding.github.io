DCB — how the suite fits together

One thesis, four products: AI-native services for accounting & finance — agents do the work, humans approve. Research grounds the knowledge, Copilot observes the work, and the firm's knowledge base learns continuously from both. Each product owns its own database; they integrate by API and entitlement, never a shared DB.

The loop: Research ingests SEC + tax sources and does two things — distills cited entries into Public's consumer KB, and feeds Practice's firm knowledge base with grounded how a task should be performed. Copilot watches the CPA work and sends redacted work-context events up to Practice via its API. Inside Practice, the agents retrieve how-to from the firm KB, do the work, humans review & approve, outcomes are recorded — and the approved outcomes flow back to continuously learn the firm KB. Two distinct knowledge bases: Public's is published & cited for consumers; the firm's is internal & procedural, improving with every job. Every product still owns its own store; integration is API + entitlement, never a shared DB. Where the data lives: the firm KB and system of record are Practice's; the consumer KB is Public's; Research keeps its raw corpus. Copilot has no server DB — only a local buffer. Practice also reads the firm's bank transactions (Plaid) so the agents can match payments to open invoices for you to confirm.
